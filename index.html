🎻 Mandolin

Italian in origin, universal in spirit — in Indian & Western styles

Mandolin (tuned G-D-A-E, identical to violin) is a beautiful bridge between Western folk and Indian classical music. Its plucked double-strings create a distinctive tremolo ideal for Ragas, Italian folk, and bluegrass.

Classical Music

Indian Classical Ragas

Classified by instrument suitability — from pentatonic beginnings to complex structures

We have classified ragas for various instruments according to their suitability for that instrument's range, technique, and the student's progression level. The goal is musical joy at every step — not just theoretical knowledge.

Teaching Philosophy: Bansuri (Flute) students begin with pentatonic ragas — Hamsadhwani, Durga, Bhupali — as fewer notes allow focus on tone and ornament. String players (Guitar, Mandolin, Violin) may begin with Bhairavi, which trains all 7 komal/shuddha positions critical for fretted and bowed technique. Ultimately, progression is guided by individual capability and musical intuition.

🌸 Tagore's Musical World

Rabindra Sangeet

Learn Gurudev Rabindranath Tagore's songs in authentic Sheet Music — for Piano, Guitar, Flute, Violin, Ukulele, Harmonica and all instruments

🎓 Mode of Teaching:  Online & Offline

About Rabindra Sangeet: Rabindranath Tagore composed over 2,000 songs that beautifully blend Indian classical music, folk, Baul, Kirtan, and Western harmonies. Riddhi Sanyal's family has deep roots in this tradition — his mother Smt. Gargi Sanyal learnt directly from the legendary Smt. Suchitra Mitra at Rabithirtha, Kolkata. These books provide authentic sheet music with lyrics, enabling learners worldwide to sing and play Tagore songs on any instrument.

About the D Scale for Violin: Violin is tuned G–D–A–E. Playing in D scale means the open 2nd string (D) is the root note — this allows open strings as natural drones, giving a rich, resonant classical feel. Each song comes in two formats: (a) compact Solo treble notation for Violin/Mandolin in Key D, and (b) full Treble & Bass sheet with chord symbols for Piano/Guitar ensemble in D scale. Bow direction markings (↓ down-bow, ↑ up-bow) are included throughout.

Riddhi Sanyal has been in the music industry for more than 20 years. His musical journey started from childhood, immersed in a deeply musical home environment. His mother, Smt. Gargi Sanyal, is a well-known Rabindra Sangeet singer who learnt from the legendary Suchitra Mitra at Rabithirtha. His grandmother, Smt. Tripti Saraswati, was a classical singer who trained under Pt. Jamini Ganguly and Sri Manabendra Mukherjee. His sister, Smt. Madhupa Sanyal, is a vocalist trained under Pt. Ajay Chakraborty at Shrutinandan.

Musical gatherings — "Ganer Ashor" — were common at home, with evenings dissolving into late nights of music. During childhood, Riddhi learnt Tabla from the noted teacher Sri Abhijit Barhouri at Malda, while legendary singer and Harmonium maestro Sri Alok Bhadhury gave lessons at home.

During college, Riddhi began learning Flute from his friend Sri Sajal Banerjee, starting with a metallic flute held in the Shehnai style. He later learnt the Bamboo Bansuri by self-practice and began performing at college functions. During his stay in the USA (2007–2010) at Minneapolis, he deepened his knowledge of sheet music at various libraries and performed Rabindra Sangeet and Bhatiali music at the Durga Puja festival organised by BAM — where his large F# Bansuri was seen and heard by an American audience for the first time.

In 2010, Riddhi found his musical Guruji — Sri D. Madhusudan — at Jadavpur. Since then, Guruji's guidance has shaped his classical practice. He has performed Indian classical music, Raga, Rabindra Sangeet, Bollywood Golden Era songs, and patriotic music at Durga Puja, Diwali, Kali Puja (Kolkata, Uttarpara, Malda), at Dasaswamedh Ghat (Varanasi), and at corporate functions in Bangalore, Mangalore and more.

"It's rare to have Indian Bansuri at a US function — it was awesome to listen to the Indian and North Eastern tunes." — Feedback from the Minneapolis Durga Puja, 2008

By profession, Riddhi is a Senior Architect at one of the world's largest software companies — 17+ years in the IT industry — yet he has never stopped practising or teaching music. He has played Raga Hamsadhwani on Bansuri to the CIO and Board of Directors of a major travel company, leaving them speechless. Riddhi loves writing music lessons for students, having designed courses from Elementary to Level 5 for Musical Academy Beniyasahakala.

His books — including "Flute for Everyone" and the Tagore Songs Sheet Music series — have been received with great enthusiasm across India, USA, Japan, Australia, Canada, UK and France.
